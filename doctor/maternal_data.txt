FOOD_AVOID:
Avoid junk food, alcohol, caffeine, raw or undercooked meat, unpasteurized dairy products, and high-mercury fish.

FOOD_EAT:
Pregnant women should eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, dairy products, nuts, and iron-rich foods.

DIET:
Maintain a balanced diet including vitamins, minerals, protein, and fiber for healthy fetal development.

HYDRATION:
Drink at least 8–10 glasses of water daily to stay hydrated.

BP:
High blood pressure during pregnancy can lead to preeclampsia.

BP_NORMAL:
Normal blood pressure is around 120/80 mmHg.

SUGAR:
High blood sugar may indicate gestational diabetes.

WEIGHT:
Healthy weight gain during pregnancy is important for fetal growth.

EXERCISE:
Light exercises like walking and prenatal yoga are beneficial.

SLEEP:
Pregnant women should get 7–9 hours of sleep daily.

MEDICATION:
Do not take medications without consulting a doctor.

SYMPTOMS:
Seek medical help if you experience severe headache, blurred vision, bleeding, or reduced fetal movement.

CHECKUP:
Regular prenatal checkups are essential for monitoring mother and baby health.

VITAMINS:
Folic acid, iron, calcium, and vitamin D are important during pregnancy.

RISK:
High risk requires immediate medical attention.
